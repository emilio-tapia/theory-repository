Recent studies show that voice assistants have become increasingly popular with 27% of the global online population using them on a monthly basis, and this number is expected to grow rapidly in the coming years. However, the accuracy and naturalness of speech recognition and voice synthesis systems remain a significant challenge for widespread adoption. According to a report by the Stanford Open Voice Network, 44% of users experience issues with speech recognition accuracy, and 48% of users feel that voice assistants sound too robotic. Improving the accuracy and naturalness of these systems would significantly improve user experience and facilitate the widespread adoption of voice technology. In addition, voice technology has shown great potential for improving accessibility in various domains, such as healthcare and education, where hands-free interaction is crucial. By exploring approaches to enhance the capabilities of voice technology through machine learning, I hope to contribute to the advancement of this field and create more inclusive and accessible solutions for users.

My research interests are firmly rooted in exploring the latest scientific approaches and technological innovations that can enhance the accuracy and naturalness of speech recognition and voice synthesis systems. According to a recent study, the global speech and voice recognition market size was valued at USD 10.6 billion in 2020 and is projected to reach USD 27.16 billion by 2028, growing at a CAGR of 12.2% from 2021 to 2028. Enabling natural language interaction with machines and devices is gaining widespread adoption in various sectors such as healthcare, education, and entertainment. A recent report by Grand View Research estimates that the healthcare sector alone is expected to have a CAGR of 20.5% over the forecast period from 2021 to 2028, with voice recognition technology playing a key role in improving patient outcomes and reducing healthcare costs. With these statistics in mind, my investigations may involve developing advanced machine learning models that can understand and interpret spoken commands and execute tasks with increased accuracy and efficiency. Through my research, I aim to help advance the capabilities of voice technology, unlocking its potential to revolutionize human-machine interaction across a wide range of domains.
